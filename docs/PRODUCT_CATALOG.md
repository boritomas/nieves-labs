## Public Messaging Rules

All public-facing product descriptions, website copy, marketing materials, and external communications must follow these rules:

### ✅ DO
- Describe products by the **customer problem they solve**
- Use **neutral, public-facing language** only
- Focus on **customer benefits and outcomes**
- Reference **industry-standard tools** (e.g., "docs, email, Slack")
- Position products as **practical, productivity-focused tools**

### ❌ DON'T
- Reference **Verizon or any specific employer**
- Expose **internal operational processes** or workflows
- Use **client-specific or company-specific language**
- Mention **internal engineering setup, infrastructure, or deployment details**
- Reference internal tools, dashboards, or proprietary systems
- Use terminology from internal automation workflows

## How to Use This Document

1. **Before launching any new product feature**: Verify it aligns with the product's stated purpose and audience.
2. **Before writing public copy**: Reference the "Public Positioning" section for this product.
3. **Before updating the homepage or marketing materials**: Ensure all descriptions follow the "Public Messaging Rules."
4. **When in doubt**: Ask—does this reference a specific employer, internal process, or client workflow? If yes, it's not public-facing.
